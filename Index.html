Types of Respiration
4 Types You Must Know
Ventilation (Breathing): Movement of gases between external environment and respiratory membrane (lung in humans).

External Respiration: Gas exchange between atmosphere and blood — at the ALVEOLI.

Internal Respiration: Gas exchange between blood and body cells. O₂ in, CO₂ out.

Cellular Respiration: In mitochondria. O₂ + Nutrients → Energy + CO₂ + H₂O
Key Structures
Path Air Takes (in order)
Nasal Cavity → filters, warms, moistens air
Pharynx → throat, joins nasal + oral cavities
Epiglottis → flap preventing food entering trachea
Larynx → voice box, vocal cords
Trachea → windpipe, cartilage rings
Bronchi → left & right, enter lungs
Bronchioles → smaller branches, smooth muscle
Alveoli → gas exchange! Tiny sacs, 1 cell thick
Breathing Mechanism
How We Breathe
INHALATION:
Diaphragm contracts → flattens ↓
Intercostals contract → ribs UP & OUT
Volume ↑ → Pressure ↓ → Air flows IN

EXHALATION:
Diaphragm relaxes → domes ↑
Intercostals relax → ribs DOWN & IN
Volume ↓ → Pressure ↑ → Air flows OUT
Gas Exchange
O₂ Into Cells
1. Air (20% O₂) arrives at alveoli
2. O₂ concentration: lungs > blood → O₂ diffuses into blood
3. Most O₂ binds to hemoglobin in red blood cells
4. Blood carries O₂ to body cells
5. Cells use O₂ constantly → cell O₂ always lower than blood
6. O₂ diffuses from blood → into cells
Blood is now deoxygenated (purplish-red)
Gas Exchange
CO₂ Out of Cells
1. Cells produce CO₂ → concentration in cells > blood
2. CO₂ diffuses into blood
3. Most CO₂ → reacts with H₂O in RBCs: CO₂ + H₂O → H⁺ + HCO₃⁻
4. Bicarbonate ions travel in plasma to lungs
5. At lungs: reaction reverses → CO₂ reforms
6. CO₂ conc in blood > alveolar air → CO₂ diffuses OUT
Alveoli Facts
Why Alveoli Are Perfect for Gas Exchange
✦ 150 million alveoli per lung
✦ Surface area = 40× your skin!
✦ Walls only 1 cell thick → rapid diffusion
✦ Surrounded by dense capillary network
✦ Moist walls allow gases to dissolve
✦ Diameter: only 0.1–0.2 mm
✦ Site of EXTERNAL respiration
Other Organisms
Gas Exchange Across Species
Worms: Skin (cutaneous) — must stay moist
Insects: Spiracles + tracheae — NO lungs
Fish: Gills — water flows over filaments (countercurrent exchange)
Frogs: Lungs + moist skin; tadpoles use gills
Humans: Lungs with alveoli
★ ASTHMA — Focus
Asthma — Know This Cold
Definition: Chronic inflammatory disorder — bronchioles constrict + excess mucus

Symptoms: Wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, nighttime cough

Triggers: Allergens, exercise, cold air, smoke, stress

Mechanism: Bronchiole smooth muscle spasms → airway narrows → mucus builds up → trapped air

Treatment: Reliever inhaler (bronchodilator/Ventolin); preventer inhaler (corticosteroids); avoid triggers
★ LARYNGITIS — Focus
Laryngitis — Know This Cold
Definition: Inflammation of the larynx (voice box) → vocal cords swell

Symptoms: Loss of voice / severe hoarseness, sore dry throat, irritating cough, fever

Causes: Viral infection (most common), bacterial, overstraining voice, acid reflux, smoke

Treatment: REST voice completely (no whispering!), stay hydrated, steam inhalation, antibiotics only if bacterial — resolves in 1–2 weeks
Structures
Trachea & Bronchiole Escalator
Trachea (Windpipe): Cartilage rings outside; ciliated mucus cells inside

Goblet Cells: Produce mucus; line trachea, bronchi, bronchioles

Bronchiole Escalator: Mucus traps dust/bacteria/pollen. Tiny cilia constantly sweep this material UP and OUT of the lungs — your natural air filter!

Smoking destroys cilia → debris accumulates → infections
Pleural Membrane
Pleura & Diaphragm
Pleural Membrane: Thin fluid-filled membrane surrounding each lung, continuous with chest cavity lining
Function: Reduces friction between lungs and chest wall during breathing

Diaphragm: Dome-shaped muscle separating chest from abdominal cavity
The MAIN breathing muscle — contracts = inhale, relaxes = exhale

External Intercostals: Pull ribs UP & OUT (inhale)
Internal Intercostals: Pull ribs DOWN during extreme exercise
Emphysema
Emphysema
Definition: Alveoli walls break down → enlarged, less elastic air sacs

Symptoms: Shortness of breath (especially during activity), barrel chest, chronic cough, reduced exercise tolerance

Cause: Primary cause is SMOKING (chemicals destroy alveolar walls)

Effect: Less surface area for gas exchange → body can't get enough O₂

Treatment: Incurable — quit smoking, bronchodilators, oxygen therapy

⬇ Lower Respiratory

DisorderSymptomsCauseTreatment
PneumoniaLower Fever, chills, productive cough, chest pain, difficulty breathing Bacterial/viral/fungal infection — alveoli fill with fluid/pus Antibiotics (bacterial); antivirals; rest; oxygen therapy if severe
BronchitisLower Persistent cough with mucus, wheezing, chest discomfort, fatigue Acute: viral/bacterial. Chronic: smoking/irritation Acute: rest, fluids. Chronic: quit smoking, inhalers, bronchodilators
AsthmaLower ★ Wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, nighttime cough Bronchiole spasm + excess mucus triggered by allergens, exercise, cold air, stress Reliever inhaler (Ventolin/bronchodilator); preventer (corticosteroids); avoid triggers; epi for severe
EmphysemaLower Shortness of breath, barrel chest, chronic cough, reduced exercise tolerance Alveoli walls destroyed → less surface area. Primary cause: SMOKING Incurable — quit smoking, bronchodilators, O₂ therapy, lung reduction surgery
Cystic FibrosisLower Thick sticky mucus, chronic lung infections, difficulty breathing, digestive problems Genetic mutation (CFTR gene) → abnormal Cl⁻ transport → thick mucus Chest physiotherapy; antibiotics; CFTR modulators (Trikafta); lung transplant
Lung CancerLower Persistent cough, coughing blood, chest pain, weight loss, fatigue Uncontrolled cell growth; primary cause: SMOKING (carcinogens damage DNA) Surgery; radiation; chemotherapy; targeted/immunotherapy
